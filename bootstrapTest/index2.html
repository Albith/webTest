Critiques

Here are three different kinds of skills we hope you'll develop in this class. There are a variety of different critiques: during our classes, we hope to encourage productive and informed discussion around different ideas, paradigms, and concepts. This kind of critique puts to use the theoretical content from ItM and applies it to examples we find in our own lives.

We'll also be encouraging studio-type critiques, where we look at and discuss artifacts, designs, and systems. These kinds of critiques are meant to offer constructive and relevant feedback for your colleague, giving them concrete and targeted advice on what elements of their design work well and which elements could use improvement. This skill can be verbal or written; an example of the latter is your design note exercise. It's good practice to flex your critique muscles outside of class, as well!

Prototypes

Prototypes refer to many things, including but not limited to: diagrams, sketches, software, or jumbles of sensors and wires. Rapid prototyping encourages iterative design, testing smaller scale ideas or concepts to see what's worth exploring further. Mockups might include user flows, UIs, or feedback loops.

You'll also be building functional artifacts in this class and prototyping will be crucial for helping everything work in the end. Both the artifacts and the sketches will be useful portfolio pieces that demonstrate your interests, skills, and personality. Whatever you build in this class is meant to serve you well in whatever environment you end up in next, be it industry, academia, or elsewhere.

Community

Through classes, labs, and project studios, the hope is that you'll build relationships with your colleagues, some of whom may end up in the same industry, or even workplace, as you. Take this opportunity to learn from your peers. You'll find that everyone has unique expertise and wells of knowledge.
